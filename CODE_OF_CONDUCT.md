# Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

ContextLock should be a welcoming place to collaborate. Be respectful,
constructive, patient, and mindful that contributors have different levels of
experience and different backgrounds.

Examples of welcome behavior include:

- Giving specific, actionable feedback.
- Disagreeing with ideas without attacking people.
- Respecting privacy and project security boundaries.
- Correcting mistakes openly and graciously.

Harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized attention, doxxing, deliberate
intimidation, or sustained disruption are not acceptable.

## Scope

This policy applies in project repositories, issues, pull requests,
discussions, and other spaces where someone represents ContextLock.

## Reporting and Enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to the repository owner through their GitHub
profile. Do not open a public issue containing sensitive personal information.

Maintainers may edit or remove contributions and may temporarily or permanently
restrict participation when behavior violates this policy. Reports will be
reviewed as promptly and confidentially as practical.

## Attribution

This policy is informed by the Contributor Covenant and common open-source
community standards.
